AI Safety Asia (AISA)

Advancing AI Safety in Asia,
starting with Southeast Asia

What is AISA?

AISA is a non-profit organisation dedicated to reducing catastrophic AI risks by enhancing AI safety and governance in Asia. It serves as a platform for intergenerational AI policy dialogue, connecting junior researchers and seasoned civil servants from Global South countries in Asia with senior AI safety researchers from the Global North to produce practical and region-specific AI safety policy research.

Why Do We Need AISA?

Southeast Asia lags behind in AI preparedness, creating an "algorithmic divide" that leaves the region vulnerable to AI's adverse impacts and missing out on its benefits. Despite significant investments from the U.S. and China in Southeast Asian AI startups, local capacity and governance remain weak. AISA addresses this gap by developing local talent and creating region-specific AI governance frameworks, thereby reducing catastrophic AI risks and empowering the Global South.

Our Goals

Enhance AI Safety Governance

Build local expertise and foster collaboration to produce practical AI safety policies.

Upskill Civil Servants

Equip mid-career civil servants with AI safety knowledge and tools to inform policy making.

Incubate Junior Researchers

Develop junior policy researchers' skills through mentorship and practical policy input.

Foster Collaboration

Encourage cross-disciplinary and cross-regional collaboration to generate Asia-focused AI safety research.

Our Approach

  • Conducting weekly AI safety tutorials for civil servants and mentorship sessions for junior researchers.
  • Establishing teams of junior researchers, civil servants, and senior AI safety experts to produce practical AI safety policy research.
  • Providing ongoing guidance from established AI safety researchers to enhance junior researchers' capabilities.
  • Translating research outputs into practical policies through collaboration with local governments.
